<p>Because of the sloppy city planning, you suspect that there may be two different towns where you cannot travel from one town to the other by passing through one or more roads. If so, as a new mayor of this city you have to resolve this problem. Unfortunately, there is not enough space to make each road bidirectional nor construct new roads. Therefore, you instead decided to reverse the directions of some roads.</p>

<p>For each pair of towns, you are given the initial direction of the road between these two towns and the cost to reverse the direction. You can reverse the directions of zero or more roads. After that, you must be able to travel from any town to any other town by passing through roads. Your task is to calculate the minimal total cost to achieve it. Under the constraints of this problem, it can be proven that a solution always exists.</p>
